About the Event

This conference brings together early-career researchers working at the forefront of quantum chemistry, electronic structure theory, and computational methods, united by a common goal: to push the boundaries of modern theoretical chemistry. The scope spans topics such as strongly correlated systems, excited states, density functional theory, and molecular dynamics, with applications ranging from biological molecules to artificial materials and solid-state systems.

Unlike traditional conferences, the primary aim of this meeting is not to present incremental research updates, but to stimulate in-depth discussions on broader challenges, emerging ideas, and conceptual advances relevant to the field. The focus is on open dialogue, critical thinking, and collaborative exploration, brainstorming and collectively examine potential solutions and future directions. Speakers are strongly encouraged to present their topics in a clear and accessible manner, making them approachable to a broad audience and enabling meaningful feedback from all participants. The conference aims to create a supportive and safe environment for sharing new ideas and refining concepts through discussions with the wider community.

By promoting open exchange and interdisciplinary discussion, the meeting seeks help with the development of stronger and more competitive research proposals, to inspire innovative research directions and stimulate new collaborations. In addition, discussions on teaching, communication, and evaluation of science are welcome, though the central focus remains on scientific exchange, idea generation, and collaborative problem-solving.

Vladislav Slama

Charles University

vladislav.slama@matfyz.cuni.cz

He graduated in Physics from Charles University in Prague under the supervision of Tomáš Mančal, with a primary focus on open quantum systems and the combination of stochastic approaches for optical spectra and exciton dynamics simulations with quantum chemical methods. He subsequently pursued postdoctoral research with Benedetta Mennucci at the University of Pisa, focusing on QM/MMPol polarizable embedding methods for excited states, with applications to biological light-harvesting complexes. In 2023, he joined the group of Ursula Rothlisberger at EPFL, where he worked on perovskite solar cells and molecular electronics, with a particular emphasis on donor–acceptor systems.
Since 2026, he has been an independent researcher at Charles University in Prague. His research focuses on light–matter interactions and multiexciton effects, ranging from electronic structure methods to the simulation of optical spectra.
At the conference, he is responsible for topics related to electronic excited states of molecular systems, electron transfer, and spectroscopy, and he serves as the organizing host for the 2026 edition.
